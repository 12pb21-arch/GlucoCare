Nutrition Overview

Eat right for stable sugar and blood pressure

Follow these daily meal guidelines to keep your blood glucose steady and maintain healthy blood pressure levels.

Daily Diet Plan

Recommended meals and ingredients tailored for your condition.

🌅 Breakfast

8:00 AM - 9:00 AM

✅ Recommended

  • Oatmeal with chia seeds and almonds
  • Boiled egg whites
  • Green tea or unsweetened black coffee

❌ Avoid

  • Sugary cereals
  • White bread or refined flour parathas
  • Sweetened fruit juices
Quick Tip: Include a source of lean protein to prevent sudden sugar spikes.

☀️ Lunch

1:00 PM - 2:00 PM

✅ Recommended

  • Mixed vegetable salad (cucumber, tomatoes)
  • 2 multigrain rotis or a small bowl of brown rice
  • Lentils (Dal) or grilled chicken/fish

❌ Avoid

  • Deep fried foods (pooris, pakoras)
  • Excessive salt (pickles, papad)
  • White rice in large quantities
Quick Tip: Start with the salad to help control your blood pressure and fill you up.

☕ Evening Snack

4:30 PM - 5:30 PM

✅ Recommended

  • Roasted chickpeas (makhana) or walnuts
  • An apple or a small bowl of papaya
  • Green tea

❌ Avoid

  • Biscuits, cookies, and rusks
  • Namkeen with high sodium
  • Milk tea with added sugar
Quick Tip: Keep snacks under 150 calories to avoid pre-dinner glucose spikes.

🌙 Dinner

7:30 PM - 8:30 PM

✅ Recommended

  • Light vegetable soup (low sodium)
  • Grilled paneer or steamed fish
  • Steamed vegetables (broccoli, spinach, beans)

❌ Avoid

  • Heavy, creamy gravies
  • Late-night sugary desserts
  • Red meat
Quick Tip: Keep dinner light and finish at least 2 hours before bedtime.
